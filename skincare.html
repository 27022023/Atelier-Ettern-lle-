Skincare Helper

A soft, champagne‑gold guide to understanding your skin, building routines, and choosing products that actually make sense.

This page is your calm skincare cheat‑sheet. It won’t diagnose anything or replace a professional, but it will help you understand your skin type, build a simple routine, and recognize ingredients and products that might fit your vibe.

🌸 Reminder: If your skin is very reactive, painful, or you’re unsure what’s happening, it’s always best to talk to a dermatologist or doctor.

Skin Types

Knowing your skin type helps you choose textures and ingredients that feel good instead of irritating.

  • Normal: Balanced, minimal issues, not too oily or dry.
  • Dry: Feels tight, flaky, rough; craves moisture and gentle formulas.
  • Oily: Shiny, especially in the T‑zone; prone to clogged pores and breakouts.
  • Combination: Oily T‑zone, normal or dry cheeks; needs mixed textures.
  • Sensitive: Easily red, stings or burns with harsh products; needs very gentle care.
🌿 Quick test: Wash your face with a gentle cleanser, wait 30 minutes with no products: if it feels tight → dry; shiny → oily; both → combination; fine → normal.

Basic Routine

Start simple. You can always add more later once your skin feels stable.

  • Morning: Gentle cleanser → Moisturizer → SPF (every single day).
  • Night: Cleanser → Treatment (optional) → Moisturizer.
✨ SPF is your best anti‑aging product. It helps prevent dark spots, fine lines, and irritation from UV.

Popular Ingredients

These ingredients show up everywhere. Here’s what they’re usually used for:

  • Niacinamide: Brightens, helps with redness, supports barrier.
  • Hyaluronic Acid: Attracts water, boosts hydration.
  • Retinol: Targets fine lines, texture, and breakouts (can be irritating).
  • AHA/BHA: Chemical exfoliants for glow and smoother texture.
  • Ceramides: Help repair and protect the skin barrier.
💛 Introduce actives slowly. Start 1–2× per week and watch how your skin reacts.

Build Your Routine by Skin Type

Here’s a calm starting point. Think of this as a template, not a rulebook.

Goal: comfort, moisture, barrier repair.

  • Cleanser (type): Creamy, non‑foaming hydrating cleanser.
    Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, La Roche‑Posay Toleriane Hydrating Cleanser.
  • Moisturizer (type): Rich cream with ceramides and oils.
    Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, La Roche‑Posay Lipikar Baume.
  • Extras: Hyaluronic acid serum, overnight hydrating mask.

Goal: balance oil, keep pores clear, avoid stripping.

  • Cleanser (type): Gentle gel cleanser, not harsh or squeaky‑clean.
    Examples: CeraVe Foaming Cleanser, La Roche‑Posay Effaclar (gentle versions).
  • Moisturizer (type): Lightweight gel or lotion, non‑comedogenic.
    Examples: Neutrogena Hydro Boost (fragrance‑free), La Roche‑Posay Effaclar Mat.
  • Treatment (type): 2% BHA (salicylic acid) or niacinamide serum.
    Examples: Paula’s Choice 2% BHA, The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1%.

Goal: hydrate dry areas, control shine in T‑zone.

  • Cleanser (type): Gentle gel or lotion cleanser.
    Examples: CeraVe Hydrating or Foaming (depending on how oily you are).
  • Moisturizer (type): Light lotion overall, richer cream only on dry areas.
  • Treatment (type): BHA on T‑zone, hydrating serum on cheeks.

Goal: calm, protect, avoid fragrance and strong actives.

  • Cleanser (type): Very gentle, fragrance‑free, non‑foaming.
    Examples: La Roche‑Posay Toleriane Dermo‑Cleanser, Avene Extremely Gentle Cleanser.
  • Moisturizer (type): Barrier‑repair cream with ceramides and soothing ingredients.
    Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, La Roche‑Posay Cicaplast Baume.
  • Extras: Avoid too many actives at once; patch test everything new.

Ingredient‑Based Suggestions

Here are some common goals and what people often look for:

  • Type: Niacinamide serum, gentle vitamin C.
    Examples: The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1%, La Roche‑Posay Pure Vitamin C (for some skin types).
  • Tip: Start with niacinamide if vitamin C feels too strong.
  • Type: BHA (salicylic acid), gentle retinoid (if tolerated).
    Examples: Paula’s Choice 2% BHA, Differin (adapalene) for some people.
  • Tip: Use only at night, not every day at first.
  • Type: Ceramide cream, simple hydrating serum, occlusive balm at night.
    Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, La Roche‑Posay Cicaplast, simple hyaluronic acid serum.
  • Tip: When your barrier is upset, pause strong actives and focus on moisture.

Extra Glow Tips

  • Change pillowcases regularly to avoid buildup.
  • Don’t scrub your face harshly — your barrier will hate it.
  • Limit exfoliation to 1–2× per week unless a professional says otherwise.
  • Apply products on slightly damp skin for extra hydration.
  • Consistency beats perfection — a simple routine done daily is powerful.
