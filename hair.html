Hair Guide

A soft coral‑rose guide to understanding your hair, caring for it, and styling it beautifully.

Healthy hair starts with understanding your hair type, porosity, density, and routine. This guide gives you everything: care tips, styling ideas, product recommendations, a hair aesthetic quiz, a haircut finder, and a custom routine generator.

Hair Types

  • Type 1 (Straight): Smooth, shiny, can get oily quickly.
  • Type 2 (Wavy): Loose waves, needs lightweight moisture.
  • Type 3 (Curly): Defined curls, needs hydration and curl creams.
  • Type 4 (Coily): Tight coils, needs rich moisture and oils.

Hair Porosity

  • Low Porosity: Water-resistant, needs lightweight products + heat to absorb moisture.
  • Medium Porosity: Balanced, absorbs moisture well.
  • High Porosity: Absorbs fast but loses moisture quickly; needs rich creams + oils.

Hair Density

  • Low Density: Hair looks thin; avoid heavy products.
  • Medium Density: Most styles work well.
  • High Density: Thick hair; needs strong hold + moisture.

Hair Strand Thickness

  • Fine: Lightweight products, avoid heavy oils.
  • Medium: Balanced products.
  • Coarse: Rich creams + oils for softness.

Haircare Basics

  • Shampoo: Focus on the scalp.
  • Conditioner: Focus on mid‑lengths and ends.
  • Heat Protection: Always before styling.
  • Trims: Every 8–12 weeks.
  • Clarify: Once a month to remove buildup.

Routines for Each Hair Type

Straight Hair:

  • Lightweight shampoo + conditioner
  • Dry shampoo between washes
  • Light oils only on ends

Wavy Hair:

  • Moisturizing shampoo
  • Leave‑in conditioner
  • Light curl cream or mousse

Curly Hair:

  • Sulfate‑free shampoo
  • Deep condition weekly
  • Curl cream + gel for definition

Coily Hair:

  • Co‑wash between shampoos
  • Rich leave‑ins + butters
  • Protective styles to retain length

How to Repair Damaged Hair

  • Protein treatments weekly.
  • Deep condition regularly.
  • Reduce heat styling.
  • Use satin pillowcases.
  • Trim split ends.

Hair Growth Tips

  • Scalp massages increase circulation.
  • Keep ends moisturized.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles.
  • Stay consistent with routine.
