Makeup Guide

A peachy‑soft guide to makeup basics, flattering techniques, and aesthetic looks for every vibe.

Makeup is about enhancing what you already have — not hiding anything. This guide walks you through techniques for your features, beginner routines, glam looks, and aesthetic styles so you can build your signature makeup vibe.

Makeup for Eye Shapes

  • Hooded Eyes: Place shadow above the crease, keep shimmer on the lid, lift the outer corner.
  • Round Eyes: Winged liner elongates; darker outer corners add definition.
  • Monolid: Gradient shadows, smoked liner, and soft wings add depth.
  • Downturned: Upward wings and lifted outer shadow create balance.
  • Almond: Most styles work — emphasize the natural shape with soft wings.

Makeup for Face Shapes

  • Round: Lifted blush + contour under cheekbones.
  • Oval: Balanced blush on the apples or slightly upward.
  • Square: Soft, blended contour to round edges.
  • Heart: Blush low on cheeks to balance a wider forehead.
  • Long: Horizontal blush placement to visually shorten the face.

Blush Placement

  • High on cheekbones: Sculpted, lifted look.
  • Apples of cheeks: Youthful and soft.
  • Across nose: Sun‑kissed, cute, soft‑girl vibe.
  • Under eye “W blush”: Korean makeup style, soft and innocent.

Contour Basics

  • Cheekbones: Under the bone for definition.
  • Jawline: Lightly along the edge for structure.
  • Nose: Soft lines only — blend upward, not outward.
  • Forehead: Light contour near hairline for balance.

Lip Shapes & Lip‑Liner Tips

  • Small lips: Overline slightly at the cupid’s bow + center of bottom lip.
  • Uneven lips: Balance by overlining only the smaller side.
  • Full lips: Soft gradient or blurred edges look stunning.
  • Thin upper lip: Overline top lip only for natural fullness.

Base Makeup Guide

  • Foundation: Use a damp sponge for natural finish; brush for fuller coverage.
  • Concealer: Inner corner + outer corner for a lifted effect.
  • Powder: Press, don’t swipe — especially under the eyes.
  • Setting spray: Use before and after makeup for longevity.

Shade Matching Tips

  • Match foundation to your neck, not your hand.
  • Check in natural light.
  • Identify undertone: warm (yellow), cool (pink), neutral (mix).
  • Test 2–3 shades along the jawline.

Beginner Makeup Kit

  • Tinted moisturizer or foundation
  • Concealer
  • Blush (cream or powder)
  • Mascara
  • Brow gel
  • Lip gloss or lipstick
  • Neutral eyeshadow palette

Tools & Brushes Guide

  • Foundation brush: Full coverage, smooth finish.
  • Sponge: Natural, blended look.
  • Fluffy eyeshadow brush: Blending.
  • Flat brush: Packing shimmer.
  • Angled brush: Liner or brows.
  • Blush brush: Soft, diffused color.

Makeup by Aesthetic

Clean Girl: Dewy skin, fluffy brows, soft blush, glossy lips.

Soft Girl: Pastel tones, pink blush, gradient lips, sparkly eyes.

Baddie: Full glam, sharp contour, bold lashes, matte lips.

Luxury: Neutral tones, soft glam, gold shimmer, satin lips.
